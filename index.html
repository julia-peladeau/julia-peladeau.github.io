PhD — Upper Limb Kinematics with the ABLE 7-axis Exoskeleton

Developed real-time kinematic and dynamic models of the upper limb coupled to the ABLE 7-axis exoskeleton. Three objectives: ensuring patient safety through real-time monitoring of joint angles and torques; optimising rehabilitation exercises based on individual patient capacities; and improving progress tracking over time.

Ran experimental protocols with clinical populations, processed multi-sensor VICON data, and published findings in the Journal of Biomechanics. Nominated for the Prix Pierre Bézier.

Imperial College London · 2021

RotoGait — Novel Humanoid Gait for the SLIDER Robot

Designed a novel locomotion gait from scratch for SLIDER, a kneel-less bipedal robot. Instead of conventional leg-swing, RotoGait has the legs rotate fully around the torso — enabling potential energy efficiency advantages at higher speeds.

Built a 5th-degree polynomial trajectory generator, an LIP-based center-of-mass predictor, a decoupled footstep planner using a "ghost" reference trajectory, and a simplified position controller. Validated in simulation across 10 speeds and 6 scenarios.

Bone 3D · Arts & Métiers / Paris Descartes · 2020

Procedural Trabecular Bone Generator for Surgical Simulators

Designed a parametric 3D microarchitecture generator for trabecular bone — producing patient-independent, mechanically realistic bone geometry for 3D-printed surgical simulators.

Extended an existing lattice algorithm to handle two strut types (rods and plates). The bone microarchitecture is fully parametrised by morphological indices identified from the literature, allowing generation of architectures representative of a wide range of patients without depending on a specific donor.

Arts & Métiers ParisTech · 2018–2019

3D-Printed Foot Prosthesis for a Child

Designed a lightweight, flexible 3D-printed foot prosthesis for a 6-year-old child amputated below the knee. Key constraint: must fit inside a standard shoe (size 26), withstand up to 800N (jump simulation), and be reprinted inexpensively as the child grows.

Full pipeline from gait analysis (Matlab) and functional requirements to CAD (Catia), finite element analysis, 3D printing, and mechanical testing. Two design iterations based on experimental results.

Péladeau J. et al. — CEA-List · Arts & Métiers ParisTech

Experimental and modelling study characterising how the ABLE 7-axis exoskeleton modifies natural upper limb kinematics under both passive (device weight only) and active (assistive torque) conditions. Established validated kinematic parameters for real-time use in control loops.
